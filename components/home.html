Google removed over 5.2 billion ads in 2022, releases annual Ads Safety Report

Google on Wednesday announced its Ad Safety Report 2022. The tech behemoth said that it removed 5.2 billion ads, restricted over 4.3 million ads, and suspended over 6.7 million advertiser accounts in 2022. In its report, Google stated that it has blocked or restricted ads from serving over 1.57 billion publisher pages across over 1,43,000 publisher sites compared to 63,000 in 2021. The 5.2 billion ads that were removed by Google were due to policy violations such as abusing the ad network, trademark violations, legal requirements, misrepresentation, gambling and games, adult content, inappropriate content, dangerous products or services, sensitive events (war in Ukraine), enabling dishonest behaviour, alcohol, counterfeit goods, etc. The 5.2 billion ads that were removed by Google were due to policy violations such as abusing the ad network, trademark violations, legal requirements, misrepresentation, gambling and games, adult content, inappropriate content, dangerous products or services, sensitive events (war in Ukraine), enabling dishonest behaviour, alcohol, counterfeit goods, etc.

Meta verified blue tick for Facebook and Instagram to cost Rs 1,450 a month on mobile

Following Elon Musk-owned Twitter’s footprint, social media behemoth Meta recently enabled users to get their Meta accounts (Facebook and Instagram) verified with a blue tick for $14.99 a month in the US. Now, according to reports, Meta Verified will cost a whopping Rs 1,450/month on mobile devices and the same will cost Rs 1,099/month if subscribed from a web browser. Just like the Twitter Blue subscription, Meta verified will add a blue checkmark to Instagram and Facebook accounts. Right now, Meta verified is currently in the beta phase, and interested users have to join the waiting list to get their Facebook and Instagram accounts verified. ChatGPT plugins decoded: Why you should get on the waitlist

Artificial Intelligence is taking over every aspect of the online experience. Last week, billionaire philanthropist Bill Gates shared his thoughts on the immense potential of AI to reduce some of the worst inequities. He went on to list how AI will revolutionise healthcare, education, and various other domains in his blog. A day later, OpenAI announced that it was endowing its AI chatbot with the ability to harness the power of the internet. ChatGPT, launched in November 2022, quickly became a phenomenon owing to its comprehensive and humanish responses to prompts. This has also made it the most used application in the shortest span of time in the history of the internet. The power of the internet has been facilitated via a set of plugins that were introduced by OpenAI on March 23. The San Francisco-based company stated that it has gradually begun rolling out plugins for ChatGPT in line with its iterative deployment’ philosophy. This, according to the company, was to assess the real-world use, impact, and safety alignment challenges faced by the sensational chatbot.
